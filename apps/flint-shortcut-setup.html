Set Up Your Flint Shortcut

Follow these steps to link your Flint Card to iOS Shortcuts. Takes around 2 minutes.

⚡

Skip the manual steps

Tap below to install the Flint shortcut automatically. It sets up the action for you — then just scan your card.

⬇ Get Shortcut

Requires the Flint app & iOS 18+. Opens in the Shortcuts app.

Part 1 — Create the Automation
1
Open the Shortcuts app

Find the Shortcuts app on your home screen and tap to open it. It has a colourful icon with two overlapping shapes.

2
Go to the Automation tab

Tap the Automation tab at the bottom of the screen. This is where NFC-triggered shortcuts are created.

3
Tap the + button

Tap the + button in the top-right corner to create a new automation.

4
Scroll to find NFC

Scroll through the list of triggers until you find NFC. Tap it to select it as your automation trigger.

5
Tap 'Scan'
🃏 Have your Flint Card ready — you'll need to tap it against your phone in the next step.

Tap Scan to begin scanning for an NFC tag.

6
Scan your Flint Card

Hold the top of your iPhone against the Flint Card until it scans. You'll feel a vibration and see a confirmation.

7
Name your Flint Card

Give the tag a name — something like "Flint Card" — then tap Add to continue.

8
Select 'Run Immediately'
⚡ Choosing Run Immediately means the card works with a single tap — no confirmation banner required.

Select Run Immediately so the shortcut fires the moment you tap the card.

Part 2 — Add the Flint Action
9
Select 'Create New Shortcut'

Tap Create New Shortcut to add the action that will run when the card is tapped.

10
Search for "Flint" and select Toggle Block Focus

In the action search bar, type Flint. From the results, select Toggle Block Focus.

11
Tap the blue tick to save

Tap the blue tick ✓ in the top-right corner to save your automation. That's it — you're all set!

🎉

You're all set!

Tap your Flint Card against your iPhone to activate your first focus session. One tap, phone blocked.
